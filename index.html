Tips for perfect installation

Which type of TV bracket should I chose?

Full motion

Tilting

Fixed (flat)

Normal use

For normal TV use, choose a standard wall mount that positions the TV directly in front of the couch for the best viewing experience.

On high

If mounting the TV above a fireplace or higher than 40 inches from the floor, consider a tilting bracket for better screen visibility.

In a big room

In a large room where you watch TV from different distances, a full-motion bracket is ideal for flexible viewing angles.

In the corner

If you're planning to mount your TV in a corner, you'll definitely need a specialized bracket.

Easy cable access

Choose a TV mount that allows easy cable access for convenient cable management and maintenance. With a Fixed bracket that would be challenging.

Price

Price varies with features, so if you need a simple installation, a budget-friendly fixed bracket may be your best option.

  • When mounting a TV, screen size and viewing environment are crucial for determining the ideal height. The general rule is to position the center of the screen at eye level when seated for comfortable viewing.
  • Larger TVs naturally have lower bottoms, so they should be mounted higher to maintain balance. For screens under 55 inches, a height of 41 to 44 inches from the floor is ideal.
