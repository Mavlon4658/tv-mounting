Mount it Once, Mount it Right

  • Prevent Neck Pain

    Mounting your TV at the wrong height can cause you to tilt your head up or down for extended periods, leading to neck strain or chronic discomfort. Choosing the correct height ensures a natural, relaxed viewing posture.

  • Improve Picture Quality

    Modern TVs are designed to look best when viewed straight on. If your screen is too high or too low, the colors and brightness may appear distorted due to the TV’s limited vertical viewing angles.

  • Avoid remounting

    Incorrect placement often leads to frustration and the need to remount the TV, which can leave holes in the wall and waste time and money. Getting it right the first time saves effort and keeps your space looking clean.

  • As professional TV installers with years of experience, we’ve seen a common issue again and again: the TV is mounted too high or too low. In fact, the majority of our redo projects come from customers who initially installed their TVs at the wrong height—leading to discomfort, poor viewing experience, or the need to drill new holes and start all over. That’s not just frustrating—it can also damage your walls and your TV.
  • Getting the height right is more important than most people think. If a TV is mounted too high, you’ll constantly strain your neck looking up. Too low, and the viewing angle might distort the picture quality. The correct mounting height depends on your room type, viewing distance, seating position, and even the tilt angle of the mount. It’s not just guesswork—it’s science.
  • That’s exactly why we developed this smart mounting calculator. It’s built to help you determine the perfect height based on real-world variables. Whether you’re setting up a living room, bedroom, or kitchen TV, our tool adjusts the recommendation for maximum comfort and the best picture quality.
